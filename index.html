§02 — Theory of change

The movement needs leaders who are visible, compelling, and culturally resonant in public.

A focused, high-touch program

The farm animal welfare movement needs leaders who are not only effective behind the scenes, but also visible, compelling, and culturally resonant in public spaces. The executive directors, founders, policy strategists, and organizational spokespeople who already lead this work carry deep expertise and moral authority.

When leaders communicate clearly and consistently, complex issues become understandable.

Policy and corporate reform campaigns gain legitimacy. Journalists and funders have credible experts to cite. The movement’s moral narrative becomes harder to ignore.

Yet many movement leaders lack the time, the operational capacity, or the digital infrastructure to communicate at the cadence the moment requires. In an era where public discourse is shaped by digital platforms, leaders must be equipped to meet audiences where they are.

Resonance is a pilot initiative built to do exactly that — a focused, high-touch program that gives the most respected voices in farm animal advocacy the strategic and operational support they need to reach their full public-facing potential. The model removes operational barriers and frees leaders to focus on what they do best: shaping the movement’s intellectual and moral direction.
